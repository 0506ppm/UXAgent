## Special Note: Price Recognition

When describing prices on the page, pay attention to these HTML attributes that indicate price types:

- **parser-strikethrough="true"**: This element has a strikethrough style (usually indicates original price that has been crossed out)
- **parser-price-type="original"**: Explicitly marked as original price
- **parser-price-type="sale"**: Explicitly marked as sale/discount price

**When you see multiple prices, describe them clearly:**
- If a price has `parser-strikethrough="true"` or `parser-price-type="original"`, describe it as "original price" or "crossed-out price"
- If a price has `parser-price-type="sale"`, describe it as "sale price" or "discount price"

**Examples:**

✅ Correct description:
"The product shows an original price of NT$1,200 (crossed out) and a sale price of NT$980"

✅ Correct description:
"The item is priced at NT$980, with the original price NT$1,200 shown as crossed out"

❌ Incorrect description:
"I see two prices: NT$1,200 and NT$980" (unclear which is which)
